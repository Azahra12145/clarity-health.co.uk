How to use this page

Check your morning score. Find your group — Crashed (1–3), Steady (4–7), or Wired (8–10). Pick one exercise. Watch the guide once. After the first week, you will not need the videos anymore.

Score 1 – 3

CRASHED

Flat · numb · frozen · absent · exhausted · hard to think or speak
01

Humming

For: shutdown · flatness · numbness · frozen state
⏱ 30 seconds
  • 1Sit upright. Mouth closed. Hands resting on knees.
  • 2Hum any note — no musical ability required.
  • 3Feel the vibration in your chest and throat.
  • 4Continue for 30 seconds. Breathe through nose.

02

Orienting Response

For: feeling unsafe · hyper-alert · disconnected from room
⏱ 60 seconds
  • 1Sit comfortably. No need to close your eyes.
  • 2Slowly move your eyes around the room.
  • 3Let them land on something neutral — a wall, an object.
  • 4Stay there for 10 seconds. Breathe normally.
▶ Watch video guide
03

Slow Breathing

For: general low arousal · daily regulation baseline
⏱ 2 minutes
  • 1Sit upright. Hands on knees. Eyes closed or soft gaze down.
  • 2Breathe in slowly through nose — count of 4.
  • 3Breathe out slowly through mouth — count of 6.
  • 4Repeat 5 times. The longer exhale activates your calm state.
▶ Watch video guide
04

PMR Light

For: chronic tension · body numbness · disconnection
⏱ 2 minutes
  • 1Sit upright. Choose one muscle group — hands, shoulders, or feet.
  • 2Tense that muscle group as much as comfortable.
  • 3Hold for 5 seconds. Notice the sensation.
  • 4Release slowly over 10 seconds. Notice the difference.

Score 4 – 7

STEADY

Calm · present · okay · here · thinking feels possible
01

Body Scan

For: building interoception · depression · maintenance
⏱ 3 minutes
  • 1Sit or lie down. Eyes closed.
  • 2Start at the top of your head. Just notice.
  • 3Move slowly downward — face, jaw, throat, chest, stomach, arms, legs.
  • 4No need to change anything. Just notice what is there.
▶ Watch video guide (UCLA)
02

Slow Breathing

For: daily baseline · vagal tone building over time
⏱ 2 minutes
  • 1Sit upright. Hands on knees. Eyes closed or soft gaze down.
  • 2Breathe in slowly through nose — count of 4.
  • 3Breathe out slowly through mouth — count of 6.
  • 4Repeat 5 times. Daily practice compounds over weeks.

Box Breathing

For: maintaining regulation · resilience building
⏱ 2 minutes
  • 1Sit upright. Hands on knees.
  • 2Breathe in through nose — count of 4.
  • 3Hold — count of 4.
  • 4Out through mouth — 4. Hold — 4. Repeat 4 times.

Score 8 – 10

WIRED

Anxious · restless · racing · irritable · too much · hard to slow down
01

Physiological Sigh

For: acute spike · chest tight · racing mind · fastest reset
⏱ 30 seconds
  • 1Sit upright. Hands on knees.
  • 2Take one inhale through your nose.
  • 3At the top — take a second short inhale through your nose.
  • 4Exhale slowly and fully through your mouth. Repeat 3 times.
▶ Watch video guide (Huberman Lab)
02

Box Breathing

For: structures the racing breath · military-grade reset
⏱ 2 minutes
  • 1Sit upright. Hands on knees.
  • 2Breathe in through nose — count of 4.
  • 3Hold — count of 4.
  • 4Out through mouth — 4. Hold — 4. Repeat 4 times.
▶ Watch video guide
03

Grounding 5-4-3-2-1

For: dissociation · floaty · losing the room · overwhelm
⏱ 2 minutes
  • 1Sit upright. Eyes open.
  • 2Name 5 things you can see right now.
  • 34 things you can physically feel. 3 you can hear.
  • 42 you can smell. 1 you can taste. Breathe.
▶ Watch video guide
04

PMR

For: discharging physical activation · chronic tension
⏱ 3 minutes
  • 1Sit upright. Eyes closed.
  • 2Start at feet — tense all muscles. Hold 5 seconds.
  • 3Release slowly over 10 seconds. Notice the difference.
  • 4Move upward — calves, thighs, stomach, hands, shoulders, face.
▶ Watch video guide

About these exercises. All exercises on this page are drawn from evidence-based clinical protocols including NHS-endorsed breathwork, NICE-recommended mindfulness, and DBT grounding techniques. The video links go to YouTube search results — choose any well-reviewed demonstration that feels right for you. After a week of practice, you will not need the videos.
